2. Information We Collect and Its Purpose

To provide an engaging and functional gaming experience, and to support our Service through advertising, we collect certain types of information. Our philosophy is to collect only the data that is necessary for these purposes and to be transparent about how it is used.

a) Information You Voluntarily Provide to Us

GameVerse is designed to be enjoyed without requiring user registration or the mandatory submission of personally identifiable information (PII) to play our core games. The limited circumstances under which we might collect PII directly from you include:

  • Direct Communications (Email, Contact Forms, Social Media): If you choose to contact us via email for support, to provide feedback, report a bug, make inquiries, or interact with us through our official social media channels (listed in the footer and Contact Us page), we will collect the information you voluntarily provide. This typically includes your name (if provided), email address, the content of your message, and any attachments. This information is used exclusively to respond to your communication, address your concerns, provide requested support, or consider your suggestions for improving GameVerse.

b) Information Collected Automatically (Usage Data, Log Files, and Analytics)

When you access and interact with the GameVerse Service, our servers and third-party analytics/advertising tools automatically log certain non-personally identifiable or pseudonymized information. This "Usage Data" is essential for the operation, maintenance, and improvement of our Service:

  • Device and Connection Information: We collect technical information about the device and connection you use to access GameVerse. This includes your IP address (which can sometimes be used to infer your general geographic location, though we or our analytics partners may anonymize or truncate this), browser type and version (e.g., Chrome, Safari, Firefox), operating system (e.g., Windows, macOS, Android, iOS), device model or type (more common for mobile web access), screen resolution, and language preferences. This helps us optimize game display and performance for different setups.
  • Interaction and Log Data: We log details about your interactions with our Service. This encompasses:
    • The specific games you play (e.g., Chess, Wooden Block Puzzle, Tic Tac Toe) and how you interact with them (e.g., selected difficulty levels, game modes, themes chosen in Tic Tac Toe).
    • The pages you visit on GameVerse (e.g., homepage, game pages, policy pages) and the order in which you visit them.
    • The time and date of your visit and the duration spent on each page or game.
    • Referral URLs (the webpage that linked you to GameVerse, if any).
    • Clickstream data (the sequence of clicks you make while navigating the site).
    • Game-specific data like high scores for games such as Wooden Block Puzzle (note: this is typically stored in your browser's local storage, not on our servers).
    • Information about errors or crashes encountered, which helps us debug and improve stability.

Purpose of Collecting Usage Data:

This automatically collected Usage Data is invaluable for several key reasons:

  • Service Improvement: Analyzing how users interact with games and features helps us identify areas for improvement, enhance usability, and make GameVerse more engaging.
  • Performance Optimization: Understanding browser types, device capabilities, and page load times allows us to optimize the performance of our games and website.
  • Bug Fixing and Stability: Error logs and crash reports are crucial for diagnosing and fixing technical problems, ensuring a stable gaming environment.
  • Content Strategy: Data on popular games and features informs our decisions about developing new content or enhancing existing offerings.
  • Security and Fraud Prevention: IP addresses and log data can be used in aggregate to monitor for suspicious activity, protect against malicious attacks (like DDoS), and maintain the security and integrity of our Service.
  • AdSense Reporting: Aggregated and anonymized Usage Data is used by Google AdSense to provide us with reports on ad performance and to help them serve relevant ads.

c) Cookies, Local Storage, and Similar Tracking Technologies

GameVerse, along with our third-party partners (most notably Google AdSense for advertising), utilizes cookies, web browser local storage, and similar tracking technologies (such as pixels or web beacons) to enhance your experience, operate our Service effectively, and support our advertising model. These technologies are small data files or code snippets placed on your device when you visit our website.

Types and Purposes:

  • Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies & Local Storage: These are vital for the basic operation of GameVerse and cannot be disabled if you wish to use core features. They are used for:
    • Remembering your game-specific preferences, such as chosen themes in Tic Tac Toe, sound volume settings, or last played difficulty in Chess or Wooden Block Puzzle. This data is typically stored in your browser's local storage.
    • Maintaining session information for certain interactive features, ensuring a smooth experience as you navigate between games or pages (though less critical for our current simple game setup).
    • Storing your high scores for games like Wooden Block Puzzle locally in your browser.
  • Performance & Analytics Cookies: If we implement tools like Google Analytics (or utilize Vercel Analytics), these cookies help us collect aggregated and largely anonymous information about how users interact with GameVerse. This includes tracking the number of visitors, a_i_sessions, popular pages, bounce rates, and sources of traffic. This data is instrumental in understanding user behavior and improving the Service's performance and content.
  • Advertising Cookies (Google AdSense): Google AdSense, our primary advertising partner, uses cookies (including the DoubleClick DART cookie and others) to:
    • Serve advertisements on GameVerse.
    • Personalize the ads displayed to you based on your browsing history on GameVerse and other websites (interest-based advertising). This helps make ads more relevant and useful.
    • Control the number of times you see a particular ad.
    • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.
    • Prevent ad fraud.

Control Over Locally Stored Data: Data such as your game high scores, theme choices, and sound preferences are stored in your browser's local storage or through cookies managed by your browser. You can typically clear this data by managing your browser's settings (e.g., clearing cache, cookies, and site data). Doing so may reset your preferences and scores on GameVerse.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences:

Most web browsers provide you with a range of options to manage cookies and similar technologies. You can usually find these settings in the "Options," "Settings," or "Preferences" menu of your browser. Typical controls allow you to:

  • View the cookies currently stored by websites.
  • Block all cookies, or only third-party cookies.
  • Delete specific cookies or all cookies when you close your browser.
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is being set.
  • Use "private" or "incognito" browsing modes, which typically do not retain cookie data after the session ends.

Please be aware that if you choose to block or delete essential cookies or disable local storage, some parts of the GameVerse Service may not function correctly (e.g., your preferences might not be saved). For comprehensive information on cookie management for various browsers, websites like www.allaboutcookies.org and www.youronlinechoices.com are excellent resources. For AdSense specific opt-outs, please refer to Section 4.

4. Advertising, Google AdSense, and Your Choices

GameVerse is a free-to-play platform supported by advertising revenue generated through Google AdSense and potentially other third-party advertising networks. This section provides detailed information about how advertising works on our Service and the choices you have regarding ad personalization.

a) How Google AdSense Works:

Google AdSense is an advertising service provided by Google LLC. It allows website publishers like GameVerse to display targeted advertisements on their sites. Google and its partners use various technologies, most notably cookies (including the DoubleClick DART cookie), to collect information about your visits to GameVerse and other websites across the internet. This information helps them:

  • Deliver ads that are more relevant to your interests (Interest-Based Advertising or Personalized Advertising).
  • Measure the effectiveness of ad campaigns (e.g., how many times an ad was shown or clicked).
  • Limit the number of times you see the same ad.
  • Detect and prevent fraudulent ad activity.
The information collected may include your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, time spent on pages, and interactions with ads. Google may also use web beacons or similar technologies in conjunction with cookies.

b) Personalized Advertising and Your Opt-Out Choices:

Personalized advertising aims to show you ads that are more likely to be of interest to you based on your past online activity. You have significant control over this:

  • Google Ad Settings: You can view and customize your Google ad personalization settings, or opt out of personalized advertising entirely, by visiting Google's Ad Settings page. Opting out does not mean you will no longer see ads; it means the ads you see may be less relevant.
  • Industry Opt-Out Tools: Many third-party ad networks, including those that may partner with Google, participate in industry self-regulatory programs. You can opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies through tools provided by:
    • The Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) at www.aboutads.info/choices/ (for US users).
    • The Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) at optout.networkadvertising.org/.
    • The European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA) at www.youronlinechoices.eu/ (for users in Europe).
  • Mobile Device Settings: For mobile apps (not directly applicable to our web-based GameVerse but good to know), you can often opt out of interest-based advertising through your device's settings (e.g., "Limit Ad Tracking" on iOS or "Opt out of Ads Personalization" on Android).

We strongly encourage you to review Google's policies for a comprehensive understanding:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • How Google uses information from sites or apps that use their services
  • Google AdSense Program Policies (which we adhere to as publishers)

By using GameVerse, you acknowledge that advertisements will be displayed. While we strive to ensure ads are appropriate and non-intrusive, the specific content of third-party ads is largely determined by the ad networks based on their targeting algorithms and your browsing history (if personalization is enabled).

9. Your Data Protection Rights and Choices (GDPR, CCPA, and General User Rights)

GameVerse respects your privacy rights and provides you with reasonable access and control over any personal information we may process. Depending on your geographical location and the applicable data protection laws (such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for individuals in the European Economic Area, or the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)/California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) for California residents), you may have specific rights regarding your personal data.

a) General Rights for All Users:

  • Access to Information: You have the right to know what personal information, if any, we hold about you. Given our current model, this would primarily apply to any information you've provided through direct communication.
  • Correction/Rectification: If you believe any personal information we hold about you (from direct contact) is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request its correction.
  • Deletion/Erasure: You may request the deletion of personal information we hold about you (from direct contact), subject to certain legal or operational retention needs.
  • Managing Cookies and Local Storage: As detailed in Section 2c, you can control cookies and locally stored data through your browser settings.
  • Opting Out of Personalized Advertising: As detailed in Section 4, you can opt out of personalized advertising from Google and other participating third-party ad networks.

b) Specific Rights under GDPR (for EEA Residents):

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have the following additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can request that we restrict the processing of your personal data under certain circumstances (e.g., if you contest its accuracy).
  • Right to Object to Processing: You can object to our processing of your personal data under certain circumstances, particularly where we process it based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to Data Portability: Where applicable, you can request to receive a copy of the personal data we hold about you in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit this data to another controller.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: If we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing your personal information, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory data protection authority in your country of residence if you believe our processing of your personal data infringes applicable data protection laws.

c) Specific Rights under CCPA/CPRA (for California Residents):

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), provides you with specific rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Know/Access: You have the right to request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources from which it was collected, the business or commercial purposes for collecting or "selling" (as broadly defined by CCPA) personal information, and the categories of third parties with whom we share personal information.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request the deletion of your personal information that we have collected, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not "sell" your personal information in the traditional sense of exchanging it for monetary consideration. However, the CCPA's definition of "sale" is broad and may encompass the use of advertising cookies that share information with ad partners for interest-based advertising. You can exercise your right to opt-out of such "sales" or "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising by utilizing the cookie management tools and advertising opt-out links provided in Section 2c and Section 4 of this Policy. We do not knowingly sell or share the personal information of minors under 16 years of age without affirmative authorization.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA/CPRA rights.
  • Right to Correct Inaccurate Information: You have the right to request the correction of inaccurate personal information that we maintain about you.
  • Right to Limit Use and Disclosure of Sensitive Personal Information (SPI): We do not currently collect information that is typically defined as SPI under CCPA/CPRA (e.g., social security number, financial account information, precise geolocation, genetic data).

d) Exercising Your Rights:

To exercise any of these rights that may be applicable to you, or if you have any questions, please contact us using the details provided in Section 11 (Contact Information). We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within the timeframes mandated by applicable law (e.g., typically within 45 days for CCPA requests, extendable once if necessary).
